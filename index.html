Touch Some Grass 𖧧

The phrase entered internet culture as a provocation directed at those who spend too much time online. To touch grass is to re‑ground oneself in nature after prolonged digital immersion.

<button> usually does one thing. You click it and something happens. Here, it does not submit, confirm, or proceed. It blooms. 32 buttons, 32 flowers, 32 different effects.

Each flower carries its own behavior. Somewhere in the trail, one feels different. Find it. Click it. The rest of the garden will follow.
